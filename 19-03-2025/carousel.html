History

The Taj Mahal, one of the most famous monuments in the world, is a 17th-century mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died in 1631 while giving birth to their 14th child.

The Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Millions of tourists visit every year to witness its beauty and history.

The first known lighthouse and one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Built around 280 BCE on the island of Pharos in Egypt, it stood approximately 100 meters (330 ft) tall. Used a large fire and polished bronze mirrors to project light far into the Mediterranean Sea.

While fewer lighthouses are operational today, many historic lighthouses have been preserved as heritage sites and tourist attractions.

Angkor Wat, located in present-day Cambodia, is the largest religious monument in the world and a masterpiece of Khmer architecture. Originally built as a Hindu temple,it later transformed into a Buddhist site, reflecting the dynamic spiritual landscape of the region.

UNESCO World Heritage Site: Designated in 1992, Angkor Wat became a symbol of Cambodia’s national identity and pride.

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, standing tall in Paris, France. Built as a centerpiece for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World’s Fair), it has become a symbol of French ingenuity and culture

UNESCO World Heritage Site: Although not a site by itself, it is part of the Paris, Banks of the Seine UNESCO designation.

The Taj Mahal is an example of Mughal architecture, combining Persian, Indian, and Islamic styles. The main mausoleum is built of white marble, which changes color depending on the time of day.

The Tower of Hercules in Spain, built by the Romans in the 2nd century CE, is still operational today.

Despite its massive size and grandeur, Angkor Wat was built with remarkable precision, aligning with celestial events and the equinoxes.

Gustave Eiffel had a private apartment at the top of the tower where he conducted scientific experiments.
